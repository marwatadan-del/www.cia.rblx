About the Agency

Who We Are

The Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) is a civilian foreign intelligence service of the federal government of the United States, tasked with gathering, processing, and analyzing national security information from around the world.

As a principal member of the United States Intelligence Community, the CIA reports to the Director of National Intelligence and is primarily focused on providing intelligence to the President and Cabinet of the United States.

Unlike the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI), which is a domestic security service, the CIA has no law enforcement function and is mainly focused on overseas intelligence gathering, with only limited domestic intelligence collection.

Core Functions

Intelligence Collection

Gathering information through human intelligence (HUMINT), signals intelligence (SIGINT), and open-source intelligence (OSINT) from foreign entities and governments.

All-Source Analysis

Synthesizing information from multiple collection disciplines to produce finished intelligence products for senior policymakers and warfighters.

Covert Action

Conducting special activities at the direction of the President to influence political, economic, or military conditions abroad in support of U.S. foreign policy.

Counterintelligence

Identifying, understanding, prioritizing, and countering the intelligence activities of foreign powers targeting the United States.

The Intelligence Cycle

How Intelligence Works

01
Phase I

Planning & Direction

Senior policymakers identify intelligence needs. The President's Daily Brief (PDB) and National Intelligence Priorities Framework (NIPF) guide what intelligence is collected and analyzed.

02
Phase II

Collection

Intelligence is gathered from human sources, technical collection systems, open sources, and liaison relationships with allied intelligence services worldwide.

03
Phase III

Processing & Exploitation

Raw data is converted into a form suitable for analysis. This includes translation, decryption, and interpretation of intercepted communications and imagery.

04
Phase IV

Analysis & Production

Analysts integrate, evaluate, and interpret collected information to produce finished intelligence products. These assessments reflect the best judgment of the Intelligence Community.

05
Phase V

Dissemination

Finished intelligence is delivered to policymakers, military commanders, and other consumers through classified publications, briefings, and electronic systems.

06
Phase VI

Feedback

Consumers evaluate intelligence products and provide feedback to refine collection and analytical priorities. This continuous loop improves the overall intelligence cycle.

Institutional History

History of the Agency

1947

National Security Act & CIA's Founding

President Harry S. Truman signs the National Security Act of 1947, creating the Central Intelligence Agency. The Act establishes the National Security Council and formalizes the country's postwar national security architecture.

1952

Establishment of the NSA

The National Security Agency is established by a classified presidential directive, separating signals intelligence from the CIA's portfolio while the CIA retains human intelligence primacy.

1961

Bay of Pigs Invasion

A CIA-sponsored paramilitary group of Cuban exiles attempts to invade Cuba and overthrow Fidel Castro. The operation fails, resulting in significant political fallout and a congressional review of the Agency's covert action authorities.

1975

Church Committee Investigations

The Senate Select Committee to Study Governmental Operations (Church Committee) investigates alleged abuses by the CIA, FBI, and NSA. The resulting reports lead to significant legislative reforms and the creation of permanent oversight committees.

2001

Post-9/11 Reorganization

Following the September 11 attacks, the Intelligence Reform and Terrorism Prevention Act restructures the Intelligence Community, creating the Director of National Intelligence position. The CIA substantially expands its counterterrorism operations.

2011

Operation Neptune Spear

CIA intelligence work leads to the identification of Osama bin Laden's compound in Abbottabad, Pakistan. U.S. Navy SEALs conduct the raid that results in bin Laden's death, ending a decade-long manhunt.

Join the Agency

Careers at the CIA

The CIA employs people from virtually every professional background, united by a commitment to serving their country. Whether you are a seasoned professional or a recent graduate, the Agency offers unique career opportunities that cannot be found anywhere else. CIA employees protect the national security of the United States by collecting and analyzing intelligence, developing new technologies, and conducting covert operations authorized by the President.

🎓

Education Requirements

Most positions require a bachelor's degree or higher. Advanced degrees are preferred for analytical and technical roles.

🇺🇸

Citizenship

All applicants must be U.S. citizens. Some positions may have additional residency and eligibility requirements.

Security Clearance

Organizational Structure

Directorates & Mission Centers

DO

Directorate of Operations

The National Clandestine Service, responsible for the clandestine collection of foreign intelligence and covert action activities conducted outside the United States. Case officers build and manage networks of human sources worldwide.

DA

Directorate of Analysis

Produces timely, accurate, and objective all-source intelligence analysis on the full range of national security issues. The Directorate prepares the President's Daily Brief and other senior-level intelligence publications.

DS&T

Directorate of Science & Technology

Research and development of technical collection capabilities, providing engineers and scientists who create the technical means by which intelligence is gathered. Responsible for open-source intelligence and geospatial analysis tools.

DS

Directorate of Support

Provides the administrative, logistical, financial, medical, security, and other support functions that enable CIA personnel to perform their missions effectively worldwide.

DD

Directorate of Digital Innovation

Accelerates the integration of cutting-edge digital and cyber tools across all Agency activities. Responsible for cyber intelligence collection, data analytics, and artificial intelligence applications in intelligence work.

CTC

Counterterrorism Center

A mission center that leads the Intelligence Community's counterterrorism effort, coordinating collection, analysis, and operations against terrorist groups threatening U.S. interests at home and abroad.

Annual Threat Assessment of the U.S. Intelligence Community

This annual assessment reflects the collective insights of the Intelligence Community, identifying the most direct, severe, and enduring threats to U.S. national security. The 2024 edition focuses on strategic competition with China and Russia, transnational terrorism, and emerging technology threats.

The assessment emphasizes that the United States faces an increasingly complex and interconnected threat environment where the actions of state and non-state actors, infectious disease, climate change, and disruptive technologies can interact in unpredictable ways.

Our Principles

Core Values

These values guide every CIA officer in fulfilling our mission with integrity, excellence, and respect for democratic principles.

I

Integrity

We adhere to the highest standards of lawful conduct. We are truthful and forthright with each other and with those we serve. We honor the public trust placed in us. We resist arrogance and remain humble in our work.

II

Excellence

We hold ourselves to the highest standards. We demand and reward the best from ourselves and each other, providing superior support to our customers in the relentless pursuit of our mission.

III

Courage

We face hard truths and difficult challenges with confidence and resolve. We make difficult, sometimes lonely, decisions in the face of danger and adversity, always acting in the interest of our country.

IV

Objectivity

We seek and speak the truth unvarnished. We understand that our assessments must be based on the weight of evidence and sound analytical judgment rather than on what policymakers might want to hear.

V

Service

We dedicate ourselves to the mission — protecting America's security and supporting policymakers in their critical national security decisions — with a sense of purpose that transcends personal ambition.

VI

Teamwork

We work as a team, recognizing that our collective effort is greater than the sum of our individual parts. We collaborate across the Intelligence Community and with our international partners to achieve shared goals.

Agency Leadership

Senior Leadership

Director · D/CIA

Director of Central Intelligence

Appointed by the President

The Director of the Central Intelligence Agency serves as the head of the CIA and is the principal intelligence advisor to the National Security Council on the CIA's collection and analytical activities.

Deputy Director · DD/CIA

Deputy Director

Second in Command

The Deputy Director assists the Director in leading the Agency, oversees day-to-day operations, and serves as Acting Director in the Director's absence. The DDCIA coordinates activities across all directorates.

General Counsel · GC/CIA

General Counsel

Chief Legal Officer

The General Counsel provides authoritative legal advice to the Director and ensures that Agency activities are conducted in accordance with applicable laws, regulations, executive orders, and presidential directives.
